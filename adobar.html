アドボ

adobo for hafus

Serves: 3–4
Prep time: 10 mins
Cook time: 1 hour (slow simmer)

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly or chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 4 tbsp shōyu
  • 4 tbsp Japanese rice vinegar
  • 1 small strip kombu
  • 5 thin slices of ginger
  • 6 garlic cloves, gently crushed with skin on
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 1 tsp mirin
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1–2 tbsp lard or neutral oil (for searing)
  • Optional: small square of dried dashi pack (to replace kombu + mirin)
  • Optional: 1 tsp coconut sugar or muscovado

Equipment

  • Palayok, or a heavy-bottomed pot with lid
  • Wooden spoon or ladle
  • Charcoal fire or low stovetop flame

Instructions

  1. Sear the meat: Heat the pot over medium-low. Add oil or lard. Sear the meat until lightly browned on most sides.
  2. Add aromatics: Add garlic, ginger, and kombu. Stir gently until fragrant.
  3. Deglaze with vinegar: Add rice vinegar first. Let it simmer without stirring for a minute.
  4. Add soy sauce, sake, mirin, bay leaf, and pepper: Stir gently. Add water to barely cover the meat. Add sugar if using.
  5. Simmer slowly: Cover and reduce heat to low. Simmer 40–60 minutes, letting it reduce naturally.
  6. Let it rest: When sauce is thick and glossy, turn off the heat. Rest at least 20 minutes before serving.
