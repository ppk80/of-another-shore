Conceptual Framework

Of Another Shore is a work of fragmentation, immersion, and multiplicity. It resists a singular, centralized experience, instead embracing a decentralized, archipelagic structure where pieces interconnect yet stand alone. The work explores how disparate elements can form a poetic whole, scattering experiences while maintaining an underlying coherence. It embraces the digital space as its site of occurence, which resists traditional sites of exhibition such as the gallery, the theater, the publication. It opts instead for a freely accessible and hopefully cross-platform experience that can be accessed at will and experienced however the user likes.

The project is influenced by intertextual, intermedia, transmedia, and conceptual works such as Mark Danielewski‚Äôs House of Leaves and Poe‚Äôs Haunted, which create expanded narratives through separate but interconnected media as well as the interactive websites in the 00s. Similarly, Of Another Shore operates across electronic literature, experimental music, digital-traditional art hybrids, and conceptual art, forming an ecosystem rather than a linear experience.

Thematically, the work explores liminality‚Äîbetween death and birth, between histories and geographies, between memory and speculation. It layers personal, familial, national, and geological histories, recognizing their interconnectedness. By juxtaposing individual experience with vast temporal scales, it asks how human presence and remembrance intersect with deep time.

Language and translation play an inherent role, not as a primary theme but as a necessity of its multilingual and interdisciplinary nature. Words shift across English, Japanese, Hiligaynon, Tagalog, and other languages, resisting full accessibility while acknowledging the fractured ways in which language and identity function. This extends to the use of specialized languages, such as scientific terminology, which highlight the limits of translatability.

Ultimately, Of Another Shore is not about fragmentation as loss but as multiplication‚Äîof perspectives, histories, and interactions. The digital space allows for this multiplicity, enabling nonlinear engagement, interactive elements, and an evolving ecology of media that continuously reshapes itself through the act of reading, listening, experiencing, and interacting.

Archival & Future Adaptations

Of Another Shore embraces both ephemerality and continuity, acknowledging that some of its elements will naturally degrade while others will persist and evolve.

Ephemeral & Degrading Elements

Many parts of Of Another Shore exist as performative experiences, which are inherently ephemeral. Future iterations may include site-specific performances, interactive events, or temporary installations that are meant to be experienced within a limited time frame. In the digital realm, technological obsolescence can occur. While the work is primarily built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, ensuring its longevity, some components‚Äîparticularly those reliant on external data sources such as weather API keys‚Äîmay eventually stop functioning as online infrastructures shift. The work does not actively resist degradation but instead allows for the possibility of digital impermanence.

Preservation Strategies

To extend accessibility and provide reference points for future audiences, Of Another Shore can be preserved through:

  • A downloadable and offline version for local access, though some works will retain dependencies on live data.
  • Video walkthroughs, which offer the most effective way to document interactive and performative aspects.
  • Textual descriptions to provide additional context and conceptual framing.
  • A maintained digital repository, with updates hosted at xyhtamura.github.io for as long as possible.

Future Evolutions & Adaptations

The work is designed to be iterative rather than fixed. It will continue to evolve, with future versions (e.g., Of Another Shore 2.0) incorporating new pieces, reworkings, or technological changes. Some elements may be expanded, altered, or entirely transformed.

Beyond its digital form, Of Another Shore is inherently multi-incarnational‚Äîcertain works may find expression as installations, printed books, live performances, or other formats. These adaptations will function both as extensions of the work and autonomous pieces in their own right, which is how poetic suites function.

Longevity & Open Access

Should the original platform become inaccessible due to technological changes, the work may be recreated in new web formats or virtual environments. To encourage ongoing engagement and reinterpretation, Of Another Shore will be released under Creative Commons 4.0, allowing future artists, researchers, and audiences to remix, extend, and transform the work.
