dungan

I was always given ginger to hide
inside my clothes. It’s to prevent
the effects of tuyáw. Many plants
resemble human features. When many
people give you a lot of attention,
you get sick. If the dungán is strong
then you’ll be fine. Lola was distraught
when I would not get
well from diarrhea and full
body rashes. After the 1990 Luzon
earthquake, I recovered.

When the body sleeps, the
dungán leaves the body.
If it’s hungry enough
it enters the rice
cooker and gets stuck there.
I have left open several
rice cookers. One by the TV
we no longer need, one
at the altar, one at the front
of the modem, one on top
of a placard that says never
again, one by the atlas
of the night sky.

Naga dungán-dúngan
and mga kalág natun.
Our souls go together


In death or illness, they’re said
to unravel. I have more
of these: one in video
essay, one on Facebook,
